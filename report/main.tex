\noindent
The CNN (88M params) and the ViT (86M params) were fine-tuned on a new dataset $\mathcal{D}$ where:\\

\[
\begin{gathered}
\mathcal{D} \; = \; \mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{train}} \; \cup \; \mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{val}} \; \cup \; \mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{test}}, \\[0.5em]
|\mathcal{D}| = 560{,}000 \,\text{ diagrams, } \\[0.5em]
|\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{train}}| \; = \; 448{,}000 \text{ diagrams,} \\[0.5em]
|\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{val}}| \; = \; |\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{test}}| \; = \; 56{,}000 \text{ diagrams}, \\[0.5em]
\forall x \in \{\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{train}}, \, \mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{val}}, \, \mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{test}}\}, \; x \in \mathcal{U} \, \cup \, \mathcal{K} \; \text{ and } \; |\mathcal{U}| = |\mathcal{K}|, \\[0.5em]
\end{gathered}
\]\\


\noindent
Each split in $\mathcal{D}$ included knots with $n$ crossings where $n \in N := \{20, \,21, \, \ldots \,, \,35\}$.
For all distinct $a, b \in N$, each split contained an equal number of $a$-crossing and $b$-crossing unknot and non-trivial knot diagrams.
